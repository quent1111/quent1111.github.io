Emergency Instructions

In case of emergency:

  • Heavy bleeding: Apply pressure to the area with sterile gauze or a clean cloth. Immediately contact our practice or the nearest emergency department.
  • Severe pain or infection: Take a painkiller recommended by your pharmacist (avoid aspirin, as it promotes bleeding) and call the practice for an emergency appointment.
  • Facial trauma (fall, blow, accident): Protect the area, apply cold if possible (ice wrapped in a cloth), and go quickly to an emergency department.
  • Torn sutures or dressings: Disinfect the wound with a mild antiseptic and contact the practice for evaluation.

Useful numbers:

  • SAMU (vital emergencies): 15
  • Fire Department: 18
  • European Emergency (mobile): 112

During evenings, weekends, and public holidays:

  • Dial 15 to reach the SAMU call center, which will direct you to the appropriate on-call service.
  • Also check our office voicemail, which may list an on-call number or an emergency dental/facial service.

Important

Any surgical procedure must necessarily be preceded by a scheduled appointment, for which you must bring:

  • A referral letter from your doctor or dentist indicating the reason for consultation
  • Your radiographs, especially panoramic X-rays
  • Your prescription for any current daily medication if applicable
  • Your valid certificate if you are a beneficiary of CMU, CSS, ACS, or AME
  • In the event of unforeseen circumstances, please inform the medical office at least 48 hours in advance. Any unjustified absence may prevent any further appointment scheduling.
